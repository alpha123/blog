#+BLOG_TAGS: faux-review plants

* highest number of members
** all habitats, every continent except antarctica
** extremely widespread
* familiar species
- sunflowers
- dandelions
- goldenrods
- lettuce
- chicory
- artichoke
- jerusalem artichoke
- herbs like tarragon, chamomile, and absinthe
- wide variety of ornamentals: aster, daisy, cosmos, dahlia, chrysanthemum, zinnia, marigolds (refers to a variety of disparate Asteraceae species)
* evolutionarily relatively ‘new’ family
** antarctica/gondwana
** oldest fossil pollen found in antarctica
** centers of diversity in south america and africa
* defining characteristic: composite flowers
* advantages
** what makes plants successful is different from what makes animals successful
- plants make their own food, and thus are not as highly limited by energy. not getting leaves eaten = more energy.
*** everything plants do is either to attract insects or to repel insects
** compound flowers
*** because "petals" (required to attract pollinators) are shared among many individual tiny flowers, the energy investment required to make each individual flower is small
the energy required to make petals is amortized across many individual flowers, and the energy required to make each flower is small
- enables redundancy
*** even if the composite flower is severely damaged there will still be individual flowers that are intact and fully functional
*** if a pollinator lands it will likely pollinate more than one individual flower
** inulin/fructans
stabilize cell membranes under drought/freezing conditions https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC2705711/
- parallel with poaceae
** high diversity of secondary metabolites
*** highest diversity of compounds in the asteroideae subfamily, which accounts for 70% of the family
https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S105579031600083X
*** often toxic and bitter to insects and herbivores
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sesquiterpene_lactone
*** many plants allelopathic
*** gold standard antimalarial drug (artemisinin) from asteraceae
* often some of the first plants to colonize disturbed soil
- extremely weedy
- reproduce fast and aggressively
- dandelions in particular are some of the relatively few plants to reproduce primarily asexually
